Exhibit 6 A Tuesday on the AML floor

Same workflow, two days. The unmeasured win is the analyst hours the framework gives back.

Detection accuracy gets the headline; the line item that drains the floor is what an analyst does between alerts.

Today · without the framework
Alert triage
Open 8 systems for one alert — TM, KYC, sanctions, CRM, transactions, prior cases — and copy-paste into a Word file. ~95% close as legitimate.
KYC / periodic review
Pull the customer dossier from 3 systems, attach screenshots, justify the risk rating in a free-text box. Annual review backlog grows by month-end.
STR drafting
Hunt the customer history, the transaction trail, the typology citation. Re-type the narrative in goAML. The investigation is in the analyst's head.
BOI refresh
Email the relationship manager, chase a beneficial-ownership form, attach to the case file. Stale-BOI list is in someone's spreadsheet.
Backlog status
"How many alerts are over SLA?" — "I'll ask the team." Dashboard shows last week's red. Board approves the program anyway.
With this framework · same Tuesday
Alert triage
One evidence bundle. Customer record, transactions, prior alerts, network neighbours, regulator citation — all chained, hash-verified, on one screen.
KYC / periodic review
Risk-rating computed from the spec, dossier auto-assembled, EDD-due list rendered live from Customer Risk page. Reviews stop being a backlog.
STR drafting
Narrative auto-drafted from the alert evidence chain. Analyst reviews and signs — doesn't re-type. Filing-latency p95 is a live metric.
BOI refresh
BOI Workflow page shows every reporting-company customer + freshness state. Stale BOI surfaces as an alert, not as someone's spreadsheet.
Backlog status
SLA timer + escalation engine. Per-queue breach-rate badges. Red queues become loud, not slide-decoration. CCO sees real status first.
Key takeaway The unmeasured win isn't accuracy — it's the analyst hours the team gets back. Multiply across 100 analysts, 50 weeks: same dollars, signal instead of motion.
