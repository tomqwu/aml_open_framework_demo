Exhibit 7 The meetings that stop happening

The other half of the win is political. When the spec is the artifact, cross-team coordination collapses.

Most of an AML program's calendar is consumed by handoffs — 1LoD ⇄ 2LoD ⇄ IT ⇄ MRM ⇄ Audit. None of that produces detection. It produces alignment, and only when the alignment holds.

Why this matters
"Examiner testing only determined whether controls existed rather than if they were actually being used."
Across the largest 2024–25 enforcement actions, the same pattern recurs: the controls existed; the coordination didn't. Internal alerts get filed and never escalated. Rule changes wait on a calendar. Audit prep starts at the regulator's email. Removing the meetings removes the failure mode.
